01 The Problem

Volunteer opportunities existed.
Finding them didn't.

The Sycamore community has no shortage of organizations that need help — or people willing to give it. The problem was that information about volunteer opportunities was scattered across websites, social media pages, and local announcements, with no single place to look. That fragmentation created unnecessary friction for the very people who wanted to help most.

📋

Fragmented Information

Opportunities were spread across different websites, Instagram pages, group chats, and word-of-mouth — each requiring a separate search with no guarantee of finding everything available.

🔍

No Clear Starting Point

Students and community members who wanted to volunteer didn't know where to begin. The process was confusing enough to discourage people before they ever showed up.

🏘️

Local Organizations Were Overlooked

Nearby nonprofits and community groups had open volunteer slots they couldn't fill — not from lack of interest, but lack of visibility in the places people actually looked.

🎓

Real Stakes for Students

For students working toward NHS requirements or service-learning hours, this disorganization had concrete consequences. Hours went unlogged. Opportunities were missed entirely.

"When everything is somewhere, nothing is anywhere. The barrier to volunteering wasn't motivation — it was the search itself."

— Insight from the Grow Sycamore team during early project planning

02 What Inspired This Project

We built the tool
we wished existed.

This project started with a personal frustration. When our team was searching for volunteer opportunities — both for National Honor Society requirements and for personal service goals — we kept running into the same wall: there was no easy, reliable way to find what was available nearby.

💡

Born from Experience

We weren't designing for a hypothetical user — we were the user. The difficulty we experienced firsthand searching for NHS hours and personal service opportunities made this problem feel real and worth solving.

🚧

A Barrier Worth Removing

We believed that if the search process was this hard for us — students who were actively motivated — it was likely discouraging others from getting involved at all. That felt like a problem worth addressing.

🌱

Technology as a Bridge

We asked ourselves: what would it look like if all local volunteer opportunities were in one place? The answer became Grow Sycamore — a simple platform to connect the people who want to help with the organizations that need them.

🤝

Community as the Goal

Our goal was never just to build an app — it was to make volunteering feel accessible and achievable for any member of the Sycamore community, regardless of how much time or experience they had.

03 Our Mission

Making it easier to show up
for the community.

Grow Sycamore exists to make volunteering more accessible. By connecting community members with local organizations in one centralized place, the platform removes the friction that too often stands between good intentions and real action — ultimately helping to strengthen the bonds that make the Sycamore community what it is.

"Volunteering shouldn't require a scavenger hunt. Grow Sycamore is our answer to that — one place, every opportunity, no barriers."

— Grow Sycamore project team

04 Our Solution

One place to find every
opportunity to give back.

Grow Sycamore is a web-based app that centralizes local volunteer opportunities and connects users directly to the organizations behind them. Instead of searching across social media, flyers, and multiple websites, community members can open one app and find what's available — with direct links to learn more and get involved.

01

Centralized Opportunities

All local volunteer opportunities organized in one place. No more searching across multiple websites or hoping a flyer didn't get buried. Everything the Sycamore community needs to find a way to help is here.

02

Direct Organization Links

Each listing connects users directly to the volunteer organization's website, so they can learn more, read requirements, and sign up with no extra steps and no dead ends. This was a core technical focus of the project — building a reliable bridge between users and real community resources.

03

Simple, Accessible Design

The app was designed to be easy to navigate for anyone — not just tech-savvy users. A clean layout and minimal clicks mean that finding and acting on an opportunity takes seconds, not minutes.

06 Technical Challenge

The feature that made
everything work.

One of our most important technical challenges was learning how to correctly implement clickable links that directed users to specific volunteer organizations' websites. This sounds straightforward, but getting it right — making sure links opened reliably, pointed to the correct destinations, and worked consistently across the app — required careful attention to our code and a lot of testing.

"This feature was the bridge between our app and the real world. Without reliable links to actual organizations, Grow Sycamore would just be a list — not a tool."

— Grow Sycamore development reflection

⚙️

The Challenge

Implementing anchor tags with correct href attributes, target behaviors, and consistent formatting throughout the app required us to think carefully about how our code was structured and how changes in one place affected others.

📚

What We Learned

Solving this problem deepened our understanding of how HTML elements work together, how links behave in a browser context, and why small details in code — like a missing attribute — can make or break a user's experience.

07 Impact & Reflection

What we built —
and what it taught us.

Grow Sycamore is more than a finished product. It's a reflection of what happens when students use technology to address a real community need — and a record of everything we learned by doing it.

✦ Social Impact

By centralizing volunteer opportunities, Grow Sycamore lowers the barrier to community service for students, families, and neighbors. When finding a way to help is easier, more people follow through — and local organizations benefit from a steadier, more connected volunteer base.

✦ What We Learned

Building this app reinforced that good technology starts with a real problem. Every decision we made — from how the page is laid out to how links are structured — was shaped by the question: will this actually help someone find what they're looking for?

✦ Personal Growth

This project pushed us to grow not just as developers, but as collaborators and problem-solvers. We learned how to break a large goal into manageable steps, work through technical obstacles together, and build something we were genuinely proud to show others.

✦ Technology for Good

Grow Sycamore showed us firsthand that even a student-built app can create meaningful change. You don't need a large team or a big budget to use technology to solve a real problem — you need a clear goal, the willingness to learn, and the drive to see it through.

08 Next Steps — Version 2.0

Where Grow Sycamore
goes from here.

The first version of Grow Sycamore proved the concept — a centralized platform genuinely helps people find volunteer opportunities they might otherwise miss. Version 2.0 would build on that foundation, adding features that make the platform more personalized, more comprehensive, and more useful to everyone who depends on it.

🏘️

More Local Organizations

Expand the directory to include more nonprofits, community groups, and service programs in the Sycamore area — and work directly with partners to keep listings current and accurate.

👤

User Accounts

Allow community members to create profiles so they can save opportunities, track their history, and return to the app without starting from scratch each time.

📋

Volunteer Hour Tracking

Give users a built-in way to log and document volunteer hours — especially useful for students fulfilling NHS requirements or service-learning commitments.

✨

Personalized Recommendations

Surface opportunities that match a user's interests, availability, and past activity — making it even easier to find a good fit without scrolling through everything.

🔔

Event Reminders

Let users opt in to reminders for upcoming volunteer events they've saved — reducing the chance that a good intention gets forgotten before the day arrives.

🤝

Community Partnerships

Work directly with local organizations to co-maintain listings, share feedback, and ensure the platform stays reliable and representative of what's truly available in the community.

The Sycamore community has no shortage of volunteer needs or willing participants. The problem was connection. Opportunities were scattered across flyers, group chats, and word of mouth, making the whole system rely on who happened to see what, when.

"I always wanted to volunteer more, but I never knew how to find anything that fit my schedule. I'd ask around and get five different answers."

— Feedback from a Sycamore student during early research

02 Our Solution

One place to find, filter,
and show up.

Grow Sycamore is a web-based platform where local organizations can post volunteer opportunities and community members can browse, filter, and sign up — all without needing a dozen different accounts or apps.

01

Centralized Opportunity Board

All local volunteer opportunities in a single, searchable feed. Organizations post once — community members find it immediately, with no dead links or outdated flyers.

02

Smart Filtering

Filter by date, category, distance, and time commitment so you can find something that actually fits your life. Results update in real time.

03

Simple Sign-Up & Hour Tracking

Claim a spot directly in the app. Logged hours are saved automatically to your profile — useful for school records, college applications, or just personal tracking.

04

Organization Dashboard

Local nonprofits and coordinators can post, edit, and manage their listings in a simple dashboard — no technical background needed.

✦ What Worked

The filtering system was immediately popular — users said it was the first time they could actually find something that matched their schedule. Simplicity was a feature, not a limitation.

✦ What Was Hard

Getting organizations to post consistently was harder than building the app itself. Technology can't replace trust and relationship-building — that took real time and real conversations.

✦ What Surprised Us

A lot of students just wanted to see that other people were volunteering. The social proof of seeing "6 people signed up" mattered more than we expected — a reminder that civic tech is still human.

✦ What We'd Do Differently

We'd involve organization coordinators earlier in the design process. A few features made perfect sense to us but added friction for them. Co-design from day one would have caught that sooner.
